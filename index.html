From the monastery to modern medicine

Ishan Shivanand’s work on mental health started long before the research or the public recognition. It was shaped in the monastery, through disciplined training within one of India’s oldest contemplative lineages, where practice was a way of life.

That formation set the standard he holds himself to: rigorous, grounded, and operating in service of humanity. Compassion Unites is the structural expression of that commitment, built to strengthen the global response to the mental health crisis.

He codified that training into protocols that modern clinical science can evaluate. They have been validated through peer-reviewed research conducted with leading US universities, and carried into the world through teaching and impact initiatives that change the realities people live with.

All of it, toward one destination: a world free from mental and physical suffering.
