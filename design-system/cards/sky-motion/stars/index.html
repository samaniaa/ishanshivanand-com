Few mid-sized stars — never a field of uniform specks. Each star is a bright core wrapped in a tight soft halo, so it reads as a star, not a dot; and each twinkles on its own rhythm — a staggered, independent pulse, so the field shimmers gently rather than blinking in unison. Rendered by .day-sky__star orbs inside .day-sky__stars; shown only in the dark phases (pre-dawn, blue hour, twilight, post-dusk).

Count & tiers14 stars: 8 bright across the crown + 6 faint accents for depth.
SizeEach orb 7–11px overall; the solid bright core is the inner ~15% (a 2–3px point).
Glow / haloRadial: solid core to 15% → soft halo at 30% opacity to 28% → fully transparent by 62%. A tight bloom, no hard edge.
TintMixed warm-white #ffe9c4 and cool-white #eef2ff (~half and half).
PlacementUpper sky only — tops 2–25%; bright ones spread across the crown, faint ones weighted lower-right, thinning toward the warm horizon.
TwinkleOpacity pulse, ease-in-out, each star its own duration 2.2–4.0s and delay 0.3–5.1s (the stagger). Bright stars swing 0.35↔1.0, faint 0.15↔0.5. Never a synchronised blink.
