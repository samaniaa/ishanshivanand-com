Sky & Motion · sun & moon

The travelling light, from the real --sun-* / --moon-* tokens. The sun is a crisp disc — warm-gold for most of the day, the orange fire ball at the horizon — that resizes as it arcs. Never white, so it holds on the blue midday sky. The moon is a thin crescent whose glow follows its shape, with two tints and two mirrored orientations.

Sun — two disc colours

warm-gold · the day sun
orange · the fire ball, daybreak / sundown

Moon — two tints, mirrored orientation

silver · waning · pre-dawn, lit toward the rising sun
cream · waxing · post-dusk, lit toward the set sun
Never whiteThe sun keeps a warm-white core in an amber body, so it reads on the blue midday sky (white washes out).
Crisp discBright hot core → solid body → glow that blends to nothing. No concentric rings, clean edge.
Crescent glowThe moon’s glow follows the crescent (a drop-shadow on the masked disc), never a round halo.
MirroredThe lit side always faces the sun: waning (lit right) opens the day toward the rising sun; waxing (lit left) closes it toward the set sun.
SizeThe sun is small at midday, large at the horizon; the moon is full at pre-dawn, a quiet ~65% companion at the close. Driven along the arc in day.js.
