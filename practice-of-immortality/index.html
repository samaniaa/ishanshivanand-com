The story behind the book

For the first 20 years of his life, Ishan Shivanand lived in a monastery, immersed in teachings and austere practices passed from master to disciple across generations.

The young boy grew up far away from urban life, in an environment which was unlike his peers’ in every way. Yet he is the most prepared to live fully in the chaos of the modern world.

The Practice of Immortality is a personal journey through discipline, doubt, and discovery. The meditations at the close of every chapter are drawn from his own lived experience.

Chapter Nine

Becoming Superhuman

मोहावरणात्सिद्धिः

Supernatural powers are a veil drawn by delusion.

Earlier, I described the way in which we are born. Before birth, we are one with God. Then we are removed from that wondrous place, and we awaken on this Earth, crying in our desolation.

95

A monk in Rajasthan once told me a different version of this story. “During the process of birth, the child is violently taken from the tranquility of the mother’s womb and plunged into this world — a place of immense confusion and chaos. The baby instinctively seeks solace in the mother’s embrace, finding calmness once again. Thus, the child surrenders to the mother, resting in a state of peace.”

96

“It is our very nature to surrender. And in surrender we find peace,” the monk told me. “A spiritual seeker is simply a person who is very cautious and watchful when choosing where to surrender.”

I have always held these words close. Unconscious surrender may be a way of giving up, whereas conscious surrender embodies the way of the warrior.

97

When my father finally ushered me into the room, he looked at me intently. “Son,” he said, “I want you to begin practicing these bija mantras and sounds.”

By this point in my life, I had undergone a change. I accepted Babaji’s guidance as readily as a flower accepts light and rain from the heavens. In this acceptance, the flower begins to bloom.

98

As the mantras continued to pour from my lips, I felt something like an earthquake from the energies they generated. A vibration passed through my body, like a thousand needles — but through the whole body.

It comes in waves, so at first you think it is just the result of sitting for so long. But then it becomes a pulse, and you realize this is something you have never experienced before.

99

That pulse became a spasm; my spine began to rotate like a conical pendulum. Then my guru touched his thumb to my forehead, and it was like an electrical current flowing into me.

Each time my spine rotated, I rotated through the universe and back. In each rotation I saw a thousand worlds, a thousand lifetimes — not experiencing them, only witnessing.

Frequently asked questions

What is The Practice of Immortality?

The Practice of Immortality is a book by Ishan Shivanand, published by Penguin Random House and Hachette in 2025. Part memoir, part practical guide, and part spiritual exploration, it distils meditation practices from a monastic yogic tradition into tools for health, happiness, focus, and inner calm. Each chapter closes with a guided meditation drawn from the author’s own lived experience.

Who wrote The Practice of Immortality?

The Practice of Immortality was written by Ishan Shivanand, an academic, philanthropist, and founder of Compassion Unites, a cross-sector mental health alliance.

Shivanand is a shaivite scholar, who spent the first 20 years of his life in a monastery, immersed in teachings and practices that have been passed from master to disciple across generations.

His breath-work and meditation practices have been studied in peer-reviewed journals including Frontiers in Psychiatry and the Journal of Urology.

What does “the practice of immortality” mean?

In the book, immortality is not about living forever. Ishan Shivanand defines it as a practice, “an art of expanding your relationship with time,” so that each moment becomes richer and more fully lived. The title points to discovering an eternal, changeless awareness that exists beyond time itself, rather than extending physical life.

How is The Practice of Immortality different from other meditation books?

Unlike most meditation books, which are either purely spiritual or purely scientific, The Practice of Immortality bridges both. Ishan Shivanand combines a monastic lineage of 21 generations of yogis with meditation methods validated in peer-reviewed clinical studies, and frames meditation as a way to reclaim attention in an age of AI and constant digital distraction.

It’s a memoir, a spiritual exploration, and a practical guide to living a fulfilled life.

Is The Practice of Immortality a religious book?

The Practice of Immortality is a spiritual exploration rather than a religious text. It draws on an ancient monastic yogic tradition but is written for readers of any faith or background who want better mental health, focus, and wellbeing, presenting meditation and mindset as a practical, evidence-informed tool for modern life.
